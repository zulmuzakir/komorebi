Hello, and welcome. This is the first entry in this digital space.

I've stared at this blank screen for longer than I care to admit. As a developer, "Hello, World" is usually the easiest part.

```go
fmt.Println("Hello, World!")
```

```rust
println!("Hello, World!");
```

Whether it's Go, Rust, or C++, we all start from the same place. It's the universal handshake between a programmer and a new machine.
But when you’re building a space for your own thoughts, those two words feel a lot heavier.

I choose the name of <b>Komorebi (木漏れ日)</b> because I like the meaning of that words: "the scattered light that filters through when sunlight shines through trees."

# So what is this space actually for?
This space is not meant to be perfect. It's a place to think out loud, to document what I learn and to share the small things that usually go unnoticed like code, anime, music, photography or anything between. 

Not everything here will be about software development.

Like komorebi itself, this space might just be fragments. Small reflections, passing thoughts, things that don’t always fit neatly into categories.

Maybe some of it will resonate. Maybe most of it won’t.

But that’s okay.

This is my “Hello, World.”

Let’s see where it goes.
